     12 ## Website
     13 
     14 [Increment.com](https://increment.com/)
     15 Well designed site that contains good articles, updated per 3 month issues. They offer a printed edition that looks good too. I haven't buy their printed edition yet, because the shipping to my country tagged twice the price of the books.
     16 
     17 [Beginner's Guide to Abstraction](https://jesseduffield.com/beginners-guide-to-abstraction/) - **Jesse Duffield**
     18 A complementary read to **Bret Victor**'s _Up and Down the Ladder of Abstraction_ (you can [read it here](http://worrydream.com/LadderOfAbstraction/)).
     19

     25 ## Apps
     26 
     27 [Boop](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/boop/id1518425043) &nbsp;<small>macOS App</small>
     28 Great utility app to process many kind of text, it can pretty format HTML, XML and JSON, convert HEX color to rgb, and more trivial stuff. It is a great tool as I often open a web tool to do those things.
     29 
     30 [Org-roam](https://www.orgroam.com/)
     31 I think, this is the end of my quest to search the best note taking app (on desktop, at the very least). I've written about it [on this post](/posts/2020-06-org-mode-with-org-roam/).
     32 
     33 [SingleFile Browser Extension](https://github.com/gildas-lormeau/SingleFile)
     34 Rotten link is one of my fear when bookmarking a site. **SingleFile** is a good site saver that generates only an html file. It's much easier and compact than "Save Page As..." browser function.
     35 
     36 [historio.us](https://historio.us/)
     37 Related to my [rant post](/posts/2020-06-on-trial-refund-support), I prefer historio.us as my site archiving service. Page caching system is better (i.e. not an `<iframe/>`), and search is easier.
